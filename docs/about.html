About

Biography

I am Professor of Global Public Health at the University of Glasgow, where I lead research at the intersection of infectious disease epidemiology, global health, and health systems strengthening. I have over 20 years of experience in researching and addressing health disparities, with a particular focus on the epidemiology of infectious diseases such as HIV and tuberculosis.

My research spans various global contexts, with a special emphasis on low- and middle-income countries, where I work on developing and improving disease surveillance systems, diagnostics, and prevention strategies. I hold a prestigious NIHR Global Health Research Professorship and lead a Wellcome Discovery Award-funded programme focused on advancing novel methods for tuberculosis surveillance and prevention. In addition, my team and I are committed to shaping global health policy through our collaborations with international organizations such as the WHO and UNAIDS.

Research Interests

  • Infectious Disease Epidemiology: Particularly HIV and tuberculosis in low- and middle-income countries.
  • Global Health Policy: Advising on policy development to improve health outcomes in high-burden settings.
  • Health Systems Strengthening: Focus on diagnostics, surveillance, and prevention strategies.
  • Global Health Funding & Programmes: Leadership of major international research programmes, including substantial funding from organizations like the Wellcome Trust and NIHR.

Key current research grants

  • NIHR Global Health Research Professorship (2024 – 2029)
  • Wellcome Discovery Award for research into novel tuberculosis surveillance and prevention methods (2024 – 2028)

Current Projects

I currently lead several major international initiatives focusing on global health and infectious diseases. These include:
- ZAMSA-TB Study — Discovering new epidemiological methods to investigate recent TB transmission and evaluate intervention effectiveness in Malawi, Zambia, and Cape Town
- AIMS-TB Africa — Investigating adaptive artificial intelligence approaches to diagnosis of TB using computer-aided X-ray testing
- BLAST TB — Understanding TB transmission in urban Malawi using whole genome sequencing and enhanced surveillance
